Problem and Challenges

Navigate the complex landscape of cybersecurity challenges with our tailored solutions designed to address three key obstacles

Cyberattacks are increasing

Cyber assaults against various enterprises, with a particular emphasis on small to mid-sized enterprises, are on the rise, growing in frequency, targeting specificity, and complexity. The 43% of cyber assaults are directed at smaller enterprises, yet only 14% are adequately equipped to safeguard against such threats.

The most common types of attacks on small businesses include:

  • Phishing/Social Engineering: 57%
  • Credential Theft: 33%
  • Compromised/Stolen Devices: 30%

Sophisticated Cyber Threats

The digital landscape is rife with sophisticated cyber threats like phishing attacks, malware infections, and website impersonation techniques. These threats are constantly evolving, causing significant financial and reputational damage to individuals and businesses alike.

Human Element Vulnerability

Despite technological advancements, cybercriminals often exploit the human element as the weakest link in cybersecurity defenses. Employees may inadvertently fall victim to phishing scams or other social engineering tactics, highlighting the need for comprehensive training and awareness programs.

Compliance with Regulations

With the introduction of new regulations such as those under the nLPD, businesses face the challenge of staying compliant while protecting their assets from cyber threats.
